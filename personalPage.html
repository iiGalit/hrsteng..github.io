About Me

I am currently a third-year psychology student at Zhejiang University, with a strong passion for interdisciplinary research at the intersection of cognitive science, human-computer interaction, and applied neuroscience. Throughout my undergraduate journey, I have gained hands-on experience in experimental design, interactive software development, and multimodal data collection. My projects often integrate tools such as Python, Unity, and eye-tracking hardware, allowing me to explore how humans interact with intelligent systems in dynamic and real-world contexts.

Outside of athletics, I also have a deep appreciation for diverse cuisines, especially Sichuan and Yunnan food. I often explore local restaurants to discover new flavors and culinary styles. Research Interests

My research interests center on human performance modeling and the cognitive mechanisms underlying human-AI interaction. I am particularly drawn to how adaptive technologies can be designed to align with human cognitive states and needs. My long-term goal is to contribute to the development of intelligent systems that are not only technically robust but also cognitively and socially aware.
