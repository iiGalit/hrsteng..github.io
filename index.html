About Me

I’m a third-year psychology student at Zhejiang University with a strong interest in human-automation/AI interaction and neuroergonomics. My research focuses on how humans collaborate with intelligent systems, using tools such as eye-tracking and physiological sensors. I aim to apply cognitive science methods to enhance understanding and control in Human-AI teaming.

Outside of research, I’m passionate about sports—especially basketball, rugby, and rock climbing—and maintain an active fitness routine. I also enjoy exploring diverse cuisines, photography, and listening to hip-hop and R&B music.

Research Interests

  • Trust, Sense of Control and Emotion in Autonomous Driving
    Investigating how interface design, adaptability influence user trust, sense of control and emotion in autonomous system, as well as how physiological responses predict these psychological factors.
  • Human Attitudes in Human–MAS (Multi-Agent System) Interaction
    Exploring how humans perceive, evaluate, and coordinate with multiple AI agents, identifying the psychological factors that shape effective teamwork, and designing human-centered systems grounded in social relationships.
  • Cognitive and Neural Mechanisms of Social Interaction Recognition
    Investigating the cognitive and neural mechanisms underlying human recognition of social interactions, with a focus on interaction synchrony and interaction patterns, using stereo-electroencephalography.
